Biography

My name is Haruzumi Furukawa, and I am deeply interested in the relationship between art and science. After seeing Salvador Dali's melting clock painting, I became curious about how visual art can express ideas about time and space. That image made me think about Einstein's theory of relativity and how perception can change depending on where we stand.

In my Media Arts work, I want to explore imagination, multiple dimensions, and the way time can feel fluid instead of fixed. I am especially interested in creating images that combine emotion with scientific concepts. Through this class, I hope to develop an intuitive artistic sense that connects thought, feeling, and visual form.

Art Assignment

This page presents my assignment work and the ideas behind it.

Concept

This work explores a moment where time appears to soften and space begins to feel unstable. I was inspired by surreal imagery and by scientific ideas about relativity, especially the thought that time and space are not always experienced as fixed.

Through distortion, transformation, and visual tension, I wanted to create an image that feels both familiar and strange. From a distance the scene seems stable, but with closer attention the structure begins to bend, suggesting that perception itself can shift.

Photography Statement

In this photography work, I focus on atmosphere, framing, and the emotional effect of visual composition. I am interested in how a single image can suggest movement, memory, and hidden layers of meaning.

By paying attention to light, texture, and perspective, I want each photograph to feel like more than a record of reality. Instead, it becomes a way of reinterpreting the world through feeling and imagination.
